Mission Statement and Values

The philosophical core of the Galactic Archives.

Why it is important

Our mission statement isn't just a tagline; it's the "North Star" for all participants. It sets the tone of serious exploration mixed with the excitement of the unknown.

The Mission

"To venture into the vast cosmic data streams of NASA and SpaceX, analyze the unknown, and build groundbreaking applications. We explore the Final Data Frontier."

Core Values

01

Exploration

We don't just consume data; we map it.

02

Immersion

Treat code as starship operations.

03

Innovation

Combine disparate data for novel utility.

Usage Guidelines

Do

  • Use the Mission Statement on all landing pages.
  • Reference values during judging criteria explanations.
  • Keep the tone awe-inspiring.

Don't

  • Don't alter the wording of the mission statement.
  • Don't use corporate "business-speak" (e.g., "synergy").
  • Don't separate the values from their numbers.

Typography

The language of the machine and the logbook.

Why it is important

Our typography distinguishes the "Human" element (Inter) from the "Machine/Space" element (Orbitron). This contrast creates a futuristic HUD (Heads-Up Display) aesthetic.

Do

  • Use Orbitron in uppercase for maximum impact.
  • Add ample line-height (1.6) to Inter paragraphs.

Don't

  • Don't use Orbitron for long paragraphs (hard to read).
  • Don't make Inter bold unless it's a sub-header.
  • Don't mix more than these two font families.

UI Effects

Immersive textures: Glass, Neon, and the Void.

Why it is important

The interface should feel like a futuristic console floating in zero-gravity. Glassmorphism provides depth without clutter, while neon glows guide user attention to critical actions.

Glassmorphism

Container

Containers float above the void. High blur, low opacity background, subtle white border.

CTA Buttons

Gradient (Indigo to Violet) + Glow Shadow + Hover Lift.

  • Ensure content inside glass cards has high contrast.
  • Use the "glow" text-shadow on headlines.
  • Round corners (1rem/16px) for organic feel.

Don't

  • Don't use flat colors for primary buttons.
  • Don't make the glass background too opaque (>80%).
  • Don't apply shadows to text inside body paragraphs.

Voice: "Stella Command"

Authoritative, Inspiring, and Immersive.

Why it is important

We are not just organizing an event; we are running a mission. The voice should feel like a briefing from a futuristic space command. It keeps participants engaged in the "game" of the hackathon.

Tone Characteristics

  • Brief & Direct Military precision. Avoid fluff. Information is vital telemetry.
  • Inspiring References to the "unknown," "frontier," and "voyage."
  • Inclusive Everyone is a "Cadet" or "Explorer." Rank is earned, but respect is given.

Case Study: The Error Message

Standard (Boring)

"Error 404: Page not found. Please go back."

Stella Command (Brand)

"Communications Lost."

"We cannot locate that sector in the archives. Telemetry indicates you have drifted off course. Return to base immediately."

Case Study: Registration Success

Stella Command (Brand)

"Clearance Code Accepted. Welcome to the Fleet, Cadet. Your mission briefing has been encrypted and sent to your inbox. Prepare for launch."

Apply this voice consistently across these platforms.

Do

  • Refer to the team as "Stella Command" or "The Fleet".
  • Use space metaphors (Launch, Orbit, Trajectory).
  • Sign off messages with "End Transmission" or "Ad Astra".

Don't

  • Don't sound robotic or unfeeling; we are human explorers.
  • Don't overuse jargon to the point of confusion.
  • Don't refer to participants as "users" or "customers".

The Logo Wordmark

The seal of the Galactic Archives.

Why it is important

The wordmark is the primary identifier. It must be legible, impactful, and always presented with the correct spacing and glow effects to maintain authority.

Construction & Best Practices

  • Font: Orbitron Bold (700 or 900).
  • Case: Always UPPERCASE.
  • Tracking: Wide letter-spacing (0.1em or 'tracking-widest').
  • Color: Pure White (#FFFFFF) with Indigo/Violet glow shadow.
  • Line Break: Preferred break after "GALACTIC" for square formats.
